About the Book

Where Oil Meets Geopolitics

I served two years as an Electronics & Instrumentation maintenance engineer on an offshore oil & gas platform in the late 1980s. This book started out as an attempt to share my offshore experiences that may be of interest to the general public—since most of us never get to visit such places in our lives.

It eventually expanded in scope. There is a lot to discuss, traversing a close to four-decade-long timeline. Using my personal experiences that sensitized me to the nuances of this massive industry, this book discusses the details of life on the platform, the impact of the oil & gas industry on the world, and the transitions it is going through with technologies such as fracking that are transforming the state of Pennsylvania in the USA, where I live now—and all the geopolitical implications that brings.

Just as an attention-grabbing example, we can point out how fracking is surprisingly making the USA the biggest producer of crude oil in the world, beating out countries like Saudi Arabia! The oil and gas industry is certainly evolving, going through major transformations along with the whole energy sector. Its impact on the world at every point, including the war in Ukraine, provides a lot of material to analyze and understand.

Similar to the oil & water that comes out of earth but doesn't mix well, there are so many countries, corporations, societies, and entities that are forced to work together, though they don't mix well or get along. We will meet them all on this journey!

About the Author

A former Electronics & Instrumentation maintenance engineer who served on offshore oil & gas platforms in India in the late 1980s.
